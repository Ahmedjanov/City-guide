This mountain is considered sacred since time immemorial.  Its surface and caves are dotted with petroglyphs dating from the Neolithic.  In general, Suleiman-Too has a peculiar shape: being stretched almost a kilometer, it reaches a height of no more than 150 meters and has several tops.  According to legend, the Islamic prophet Suleiman is buried here, somewhere on the stones there are prints of his forehead and knees.  There is also a chapel 15 House of Babur.  A path leads up the mountain — a test of loyalty for women.  In ancient times, a faithful wife who reached the summit could ask her husband for the birth of a child.
